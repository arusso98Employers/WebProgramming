The Herons of Maryland

Introduction

Herons are birds that are usually found near water. In general, they have long necks, legs, and beak that help them fish from the water. Herons belong to the family Ardeidae.
When herons fly, they retract their necks into an S-shape.This can only help you distingush a heron from other similar looking birds like storks or cranes. There are about 60 species of herons in the world. Fortunely we dont have to travel far from home to see a heron. Several species of heron can be found right here in Maryland. Just keep your eyes open the next time you are near a bond, a creek, or a lake.

Green Heron

Green herons are very smart birds- they can use tools! (Attribution: by basar from wikipedia commons)

According to the Cornell lab of Omithology - All About Birds Website:

The green heron is one of the world's few tool using bird-species.
It creates fishing lures with bread crusts, insects, earthworms, twigs, feathers, and other objects, dropping them on the surface of the water to entice small fish
